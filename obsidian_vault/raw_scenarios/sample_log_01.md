# Raw Scenario Log — Sample 01

**Date:** 2026-04-08
**Shift:** Morning (7:00 AM – 11:00 AM)
**Staff Present:** Adam, Lavanya
**Conditions:** Overcast. Register printer jammed twice. Oat milk running low by 8:30.

---

## Shift Log

**7:02 AM** — Adam arrives four minutes late. Doesn't mention it. Starts calibrating the grinder without being asked.

**7:18 AM** — First customers. Two regulars: Marcus (black drip, no room) and the woman with the dog who always ties it up wrong and comes back to fix it mid-order.

**7:45 AM** — Rush begins. Ticket queue backs up to seven drinks within six minutes. Adam calls out orders with unusual calm. Lavanya is on register and bar simultaneously. No words exchanged between them for 22 minutes — just motion, handoffs, a rhythm that looks choreographed.

**8:12 AM** — A man in a Lockheed polo orders a half-caf oat milk lavender latte, then clarifies he wants it "not too sweet." Adam pauses for exactly one second before responding "of course." Lavanya, with her back turned, says nothing. Her shoulders do something.

**8:31 AM** — Oat milk supply hits critical. Adam texts the group chat without breaking stride. Lavanya checks the cooler, confirms, and makes eye contact with Adam across the bar for the first time all morning. He nods. She nods. The oat milk situation is contained.

**8:47 AM** — The printer jams mid-ticket. Three orders go into verbal limbo. A customer at the end of the queue sighs loudly and checks his watch. Lavanya smiles at him. The smile does not reach her eyes.

**9:03 AM** — Rush peaks and breaks. Adam wipes down the steam wand with more focus than the task requires. Lavanya counts the till drawer, off by two dollars, finds it, writes nothing down.

**9:22 AM** — The Lockheed polo man returns. His drink was "a little too sweet." Adam remakes it without comment. Lavanya watches from the prep area.

**9:41 AM** — First real lull. Adam makes himself a cortado and stands at the end of the bar. Lavanya refills the pastry case. Neither speaks for a while. Then she asks, without looking up: *"How's the warehouse thing going?"* He says: *"Close."* She nods. That's it.

**10:15 AM** — Steady trickle. Uneventful except for a toddler who knocked over a display of locally-made honey jars. No jars broke. Everyone involved acted like they did.

**10:58 AM** — Shift winds down. Adam is already back in his head — Lavanya can tell by the way he stops narrating orders out loud. She clocks out two minutes early. He doesn't notice, or doesn't say so.

---

## Notes

- Tension felt high early, not because of conflict — more like voltage with nowhere to go.
- The "close" exchange at 9:41 felt significant in context. Unclear why.
- Lockheed polo man has now visited four times this week. He is becoming a recurring element.
