# Setting: Qahwtea

**Type:** Specialty Coffee Cafe
**Location:** Huntsville, Alabama

## Overview

Qahwtea (pronounced like "qahwa" — Arabic for coffee — blended with "tea") sits on a corner in Huntsville's tech corridor, wedged between a coworking space and a lunch spot that's always just about to close. The name confuses first-timers. Regulars consider this a rite of passage.

## The Space

Exposed brick. Mismatched chairs. A hand-lettered menu board that gets updated with passive-aggressive frequency. The espresso machine is a La Marzocca that cost more than some of the regulars' cars and is treated accordingly. The wifi is fast, the outlets are plentiful, and the music is always slightly too good for the ambient noise level.

## Rush Hour Chaos

Between 7:45 and 9:15 AM, Qahwtea becomes a different place entirely. The Cummings Research Park crowd hits in waves — aerospace engineers, defense contractors, startup founders, NASA adjacents — all of them under-caffeinated and overcommitted. Orders stack. Tickets blur. The espresso machine runs hot. Someone always orders a half-caf oat milk lavender latte during the worst possible window.

This is the crucible. This is where characters are revealed.

## Atmosphere Notes

- The afternoon lull is deceptively quiet — this is when things are actually said.
- There is a corner table (Table 7) that regulars orbit like it means something. It does.
- The back prep area is the only place staff can speak candidly. It has become, functionally, the confessional booth.
